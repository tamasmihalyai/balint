Practical access solution

When scaffolding is too much for a targeted job

For many small or focused maintenance tasks, full scaffolding can mean extra cost, delay and disruption. Where the building and task are suitable, rope access can provide a more efficient way to inspect, clean or repair the exact problem area.

  • Useful for targeted facade, roof and gutter work
  • Can reduce disruption for residents, guests and tenants
  • Allows difficult areas to be assessed before the issue becomes larger
  • Suitability is assessed honestly before the work is confirmed

Common questions before requesting height work

Practical answers for property managers, hotels and commercial building clients considering facade, roof, gutter, cleaning or repair work in difficult-to-access areas.

Do you work with property managers and commercial buildings?

Yes. Graz Höhenarbeiten supports property managers, hotels, commercial property owners and facility teams that need safe, practical help with difficult-to-access building areas.

Can work often be completed without scaffolding?

In many cases, rope access can be a practical alternative to scaffolding, especially for targeted facade, roof, gutter, cleaning or repair tasks. The safest and most suitable access method is confirmed after the building and task have been assessed.

Which areas do you serve?

Graz Höhenarbeiten serves Graz, Graz-Umgebung and selected areas across Styria. For projects outside Graz, availability depends on the type of work, access requirements and project scope.

What types of height work do you carry out?

Typical work includes facade repairs and cleaning, gutter cleaning and repairs, roof cleaning, minor roof leak support, high-level window cleaning and general building maintenance where normal access is difficult.

Can you assess the building before quoting?

Yes. For most jobs, an initial assessment is needed to understand the building, access points, safety requirements and the exact scope of work before a clear quote can be provided.

What information should I send with an enquiry?

Please send the building location, a short description of the issue, photos if available, the approximate height or floor level, any known access details and how urgent the work is.
